Understanding the issue

The landscape
we're up against

India is among the world's largest consumers of ballpoint pens. Nearly all are made from polypropylene and polystyrene — plastics that do not biodegrade in any meaningful human timeframe.

Pens are consistently overlooked in recycling conversations. Their small size makes mechanical sorting impossible. They accumulate in landfills or waterways, feeding the growing microplastic crisis.

Institutions — schools, universities, offices — generate the most pen waste, yet almost none have structured mechanisms to divert it from landfill.
